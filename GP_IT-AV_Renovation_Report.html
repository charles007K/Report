Why Active Speakers Hum

Active speakers have built-in amplifiers that share the same AC circuit as computers, monitors, and AV equipment. This creates a ground loop — a difference in ground potential between devices that induces a 50/60Hz current into the audio signal chain, heard as a constant buzz or hum.

The hum is NOT a speaker defect — it is a system-level electrical isolation problem. Even new active speakers would hum in this configuration. The fix is architectural: separate the amplification from shared power circuits.
